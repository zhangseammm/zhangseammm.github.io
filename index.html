GaussianSpa: An “Optimizing-Sparsifying” Simplification Framework for Compact and High-Quality Gaussian Splatting

Anonymous1, Anonymous2, Anonymous2, Anonymous1,2,
1University of Anonymous 2Anonymous
Paper arXiv Video Code Data

Abstract

We present GaussianSpa, enabling high-quality and compact view synthesis with superior rendering of details.

3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) has emerged as a mainstream for novel view synthesis, leveraging continuous aggregations of Gaussian functions to model scene geometry. However, 3DGS suffers from substantial memory requirements to store the multitude of Gaussians, hindering its practicality. To address this challenge, we introduce GaussianSpa, an optimization-based simplification framework for compact and high-quality 3DGS. Specifically, we formulate the simplification as an optimization problem associated with the 3DGS training. Correspondingly, we propose an efficient "optimizing-sparsifying" solution that alternatively solves two independent sub-problems, gradually imposing strong sparsity onto the Gaussians in the training process.

Our comprehensive evaluations on various datasets show the superiority of GaussianSpa over the state-of-the-arts. Notably, Our GaussianSpa achieves an average PSNR improvement of 0.9 dB with 10x fewer Gaussians compared to the vanilla 3DGS.

Method Overview

Our method consists of three main steps: (more details please refer to our paper)

  1. The constrained numbers of Gaussians optimization problem can be reformulated as: \begin{equation}\label{eqn:obj-opacity-form} \begin{aligned} \min_{(a,\Theta)} ~&\mathcal{L}(a,\Theta), \text{s.t.}&\|a\|_0\leq\kappa. \end{aligned} \end{equation} Variable \(\Theta\) represent other GS variables except for the opacity \(a\), and restrict opacity number of non-zeros with \(\ell_0\) norm.
  2. \begin{equation}\label{eqn:obj-optimizing} \hspace{-1.7mm}\textbf{Optimizing Step:}\min_{a,\Theta}\mathcal{L}(a,\Theta)+\frac{\delta}{2}\|a-z+\lambda\|^2. \end{equation} The "optimizing" step essentially optimizes the original Gaussian and pushes the opacity \(a\) close to the auxiliary \(z\) that is exactly sparse using a gradient-based approach. This way, it can simultaneously satisfy the 3DGS performance and the sparsity requirements, achieving a sweet point in the training process.
  3. \begin{equation}\label{eqn:obj-sparsifying} \textbf{Sparsifying Step:}\min_{z}h(z)+\frac{\delta}{2}\|a-z+\lambda\|^2. \end{equation} The "sparsifying" step essentially prunes the auxiliary variable \(z\), which can be considered the exactly sparse version of \(a\). By not directly operating the original Gaussians, this step avoids the irreversible loss of important information.
